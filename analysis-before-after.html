Executive Summary

The Problem: Manual Configuration Doesn't Scale

Today, the Taboola feed on t-online.de is configured through manually-set custom properties and custom UI modes managed in Backstage. This requires account managers to visually inspect the publisher site, manually extract colors, fonts, and spacing values, then configure each property individually. The result is often a feed that approximates but doesn't precisely match the publisher's brand — with drift accumulating over time as the publisher evolves their design.

Key Advantages

Why Crawler-Generated Brand Kits Win

Moving from manual custom properties to automated brand kit extraction provides compounding benefits across accuracy, scale, and maintenance.

🎯

Pixel-Perfect Accuracy

The crawler extracts actual computed CSS values from the live DOM — not approximations from eyedropping screenshots. t-online's #171B26 near-black is captured exactly, not rounded to #333333. Font stacks include the real fallback chain.

🔀

Semantic Richness

Custom properties are flat key-value pairs. A brand kit JSON captures meaning: the magenta dot is a brand mark motif appended to every section heading. Opinion content uses serif italic. This lets the feed render content-type-aware cards that no flat property list can express.

⚡

Setup in Minutes, Not Hours

A full brand kit for t-online — covering colors, fonts, type scale, layout patterns, iconography, photo style, and brand voice — was generated in under 5 minutes. Manual configuration of just the basic properties takes 4–6 hours and still misses semantic tokens.

🔄

Auto-Sync on Redesign

When t-online updates their nav, shifts a color, or adds a new content type, a re-crawl captures it immediately. Manual properties go stale silently — the feed drifts from the publisher's design with no alert, no diff, no audit trail.

📈

Scales to 3,500+ Publishers

Taboola serves 3,500+ publishers. Manually configuring custom properties for each is an enormous ongoing cost. Automated crawling can generate and maintain brand kits for the entire publisher network in a single scheduled pipeline run.

🛡

Captures What Properties Can't

Manual custom properties can set fontFamily and titleColor. They cannot express: "opinion headlines use a different font family than news," "section headings have a magenta dot appended," or "video thumbnails show a magenta play icon at top-right." Brand kit JSON can.

Gap Analysis

What the Current Feed Gets Wrong on t-online

Specific brand fidelity gaps observed in the current Taboola feed below the article on the t-online spritpreise article page, mapped to their brand kit JSON fix.

Gap Current Behavior Brand Kit Solution
Wrong font family Feed renders in Arial/system sans-serif. Looks generic and visually disconnected from the article body above, which uses Roboto. fonts.primary.family: "Roboto" with fallbacks: ["Arial", "sans-serif"] — seamless transition from article to feed.
Wrong text color hierarchy Headlines use #333, source labels use #999 — both are generic grays that don't match t-online's blue-gray palette. Three-tier system: #171B26 (headlines) → #474C59 (labels) → #8D93A6 (meta). Directly from brand kit.
Missing magenta dot motif Section heading "Mehr für dich" has no brand mark. Looks like a generic Taboola widget, not a native t-online section. logos.brand_mark: every section heading gets ::after { content: "."; color: #E20074 } — instantly recognizable as t-online.
No opinion/editorial distinction All cards look identical. No way to tell opinion content from news. Undermines t-online's journalistic credibility model. brand_voice.content_distinction: opinion cards get "MEINUNG" label in magenta + RobotoSerif italic headline. Critical for trust.
No video play indicators Video content thumbnails look identical to article thumbnails. Users don't know they'll get a video until they click. photo_style.video_thumbnails: magenta play icon at top-right of video cards, matching the rest of t-online.
Wrong accent/link color Attribution text uses Material Blue (#2196F3) — a Google design system color completely alien to t-online. colors.primary.hex: "#E20074" for all interactive accents. Hover states, link arrows, active indicators.
No category labels on cards Cards show only headline + source. No "Wahl in Baden-Württemberg" or "Bedrohte Tierart" category context above headlines. headline_style.pattern: "topic colon" pattern replicated in feed. Category labels in #474C59 above headlines.
Generic border/divider colors #E0E0E0 warm gray dividers clash with t-online's cool blue-gray palette. colors.backgrounds.secondary.hex: "#EBEFF7" — the exact cool blue-gray t-online uses everywhere.
